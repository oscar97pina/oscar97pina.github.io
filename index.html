About Me

My name is Oscar Pina and I was born in Barcelona on September, 1997. I am a second year Ph.D. student at Technical University of Catalonia (UPC) supervised by Dr. Verónica Vilaplana. My research interests are self-supervised learning of representations, geometric deep learning and medical image analysis . My research is focused on self-supervised learning on graphs and its application to the fascinating field of computational pathology. The work is supported by AGAUR, which awarded me with a 3 year long grant. I am also a teacher assistant at UPC, where I am teaching deep learning and computer vision courses at both graduate and undergraduate levels.

Publications

2022

Self-supervised graph representations of WSIs

GeoMedIA 2022, Amsterdam

Oscar Pina, Verónica Vilaplana

We propose a framework for the analysis of whole slide images (WSI) on the cell entity space with self-supervised deep learning on graphs and explore its representation quality at different levels of application. We encode local cell graphs, which can be seen as image regions, with a self-supervised GNN encoder and represent the WSI as a graph of encoded regions.

2022

Structural networks for brain age prediction

MIDL 2022, Zurich

Oscar Pina, Irene Cumplido-Mayoral, Raffaele Cacciaglia, José María González-de-Echávarri, Juan Domingo Gispert, Verónica Vilaplana

In this work we analyze the implementation of geometric models with sMRI by building graphs of ROIs (ROI graphs) using tools from Graph Signal Processing literature and evaluate their performance in a downstream supervised task, age prediction.
